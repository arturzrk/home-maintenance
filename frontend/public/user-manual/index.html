Home Maintenance — User Guide

Track maintenance for every property you own: one-off jobs and recurring schedules, each with its own step-by-step checklist.

Covers the current web app. Sign-in is by Google account; there is no separate registration step.

Getting started

Open the app in a browser. If you're not signed in, you'll land on the sign-in page.

  1. Click Sign in with Google.
  2. Approve the Google permission prompt.
  3. You're dropped onto My properties — or, if you followed a direct link to a specific property or job, straight to that page.

There's no username/password option and no separate sign-up — your Google account is your account. Sign in again any time your session expires.

Your data is private to you Each Google account is its own separate owner. Properties, jobs, and history tied to your account are never visible to anyone else, and there's currently no way to share a property or invite a collaborator.
If you see an amber sign-in box instead Some development/test copies of the app show an amber "development stub" box with an "OwnerId" field instead of the Google button. That's a developer testing shortcut, not part of the normal app — production instances always use Google sign-in.

Key concepts

Six ideas cover the whole app:

TermWhat it means
PropertyA home or building you're tracking. Just a name — create as many as you own.
AssetA thing at a property that maintenance revolves around — a boiler, the gutters, a lawn mower. Has a name, an optional category and notes. Jobs and recurring schedules can optionally be tied to one.
JobA single maintenance task on a property, e.g. "Replace furnace filter." Has a name, an optional due date, a status, and a checklist of steps.
Recurring schedule (job definition)A template that automatically creates new jobs on a repeating schedule, e.g. "Service boiler, every 6 months."
StepOne checklist item on a job — a description and a checkbox.
ScheduleHow often a recurring job repeats: a unit (day/week/month/year), a multiplier ("every 3 months"), a start date, and an optional end date.

A job either stands alone (you created it directly) or traces back to a recurring schedule that generated it — either way, it works the same once created.

Properties

Create a property

On My properties, type a name into the Property name field and click Create (it briefly reads Creating…). The name is required and capped at 100 characters.

If you have no properties yet, the page simply reads: "No properties yet. Create one above to get started."

Open a property

Click any property card to open its detail page. This is where you manage that property's recurring schedules and jobs.

Rename a property

Click directly on the property name to edit it in place: type the new name, then press Enter or click elsewhere to save, or Esc to cancel. If saving fails, the field reverts and shows an inline error.

No delete Properties can't be deleted once created. Pick names you'll be happy to keep.

Assets

An asset is a thing at a property you maintain — the boiler, the gutters, a lawn mower. Assets are optional: you can keep using jobs and schedules without them, but tying work to an asset gives you a single page showing everything ever done (and scheduled) for that item.

Create an asset

On a property page, scroll to the Assets section and use the Add asset form: type a Name (required), optionally a Category (e.g. "Heating"), and click Add asset (Adding… while it saves). The new asset appears as a card in the section. If the property has no assets yet, the section reads: "No assets yet."

The asset detail page

Click an asset card to open its page. There you can:

  • Rename it — click the name to edit inline, same as a property name.
  • Edit its category and notes — click the Category: or Notes: value to edit inline. Clearing the text removes it (shown as "No category" / "No notes").
  • See its work — a Jobs list and a Recurring jobs list showing everything tied to this asset, past and upcoming.

Tie work to an asset

Once a property has at least one asset, the Create job and Add recurring job forms show an extra Asset (optional) dropdown, defaulting to "No asset." Pick an asset when creating the work and it's linked from then on:

  • The job's page (and the schedule's page) shows an Asset: link back to the asset.
  • Jobs generated by an asset-tied recurring schedule inherit the same asset automatically.
  • The link is set at creation time only — you can't attach, change, or remove an asset on existing work.

Retire an asset (mark obsolete)

When an item is scrapped or replaced, open its page and click Mark obsolete. An obsolete asset:

  • shows an Obsolete badge on its card and detail page;
  • disappears from the Asset (optional) dropdowns, so no new work can be tied to it;
  • keeps its full history — existing jobs and schedules stay linked and unaffected, and the detail page keeps working.

Changed your mind? Click Reactivate on the asset's page and it becomes available for new work again.

Obsolete instead of delete Assets can't be deleted — marking one obsolete is the intended way to retire it. That keeps the maintenance history of the item intact forever, and it's fully reversible.

Recurring maintenance schedules

Use a recurring schedule for anything that repeats — filter changes, boiler servicing, gutter cleaning.

Create a recurring schedule

On a property page, click + Add recurring job to open the Add recurring job form:

  • Name — e.g. "Service boiler"
  • Every [ number ] [ Day / Week / Month / Year ] — how often it repeats
  • Start date — required
  • End date — optional; if set, must be after the start date
  • Asset (optional) — shown only if the property has assets; every job the schedule generates is tied to the chosen asset (see Assets)
  • Steps (optional) — add rows with + Add step, remove a row with its Remove button

Click Save recurring job (Saving… while it saves), or Cancel to back out. Once saved, the schedule is shown in plain English, e.g. "Every month from Jun 2026" or "Every 3 months from Jun 2026."

Edit a schedule

The name is inline-editable, same as a property name. Click Edit schedule to reopen the cadence/date fields and adjust them, then Save or Cancel.

Edit the step list (step templates)

Reorder rows with the up/down buttons (there's no drag-and-drop), edit a description inline, or remove a row with Remove. Add a new one via the Add a step template field and its Add button.

Step template edits are forward-only Changing the step templates only affects jobs generated after the change. Jobs already created keep whatever steps they were given when they were generated — editing the template won't retroactively update them.

Generating jobs

The app automatically generates upcoming jobs from each schedule in the background (typically a few months ahead), so you don't need to do anything for jobs to keep appearing on time. If you want to pull the next occurrence forward yourself, open the schedule and click Generate next (Generating… while it works) — this takes you straight to the new job. If the next occurrence already exists, you'll see: "The next occurrence is already scheduled."

No pause, archive, or delete A recurring schedule stays active indefinitely once created (unless you gave it an end date). There's no way to pause or remove one — only its steps can be edited or removed.

One-off jobs

For a task that won't repeat, skip the schedule and create a job directly. On a property page, use the always-visible Create job form:

  • Name — e.g. "Service boiler"
  • Due date (optional)
  • Asset (optional) — shown only if the property has assets; ties the job to one (see Assets)
  • Steps (one per line, optional) — a text box; each line becomes its own checklist step, e.g.: 
    Shut off gas
Drain system
Replace filter

Click Create job (Creating… while it saves) and you'll land on the new job's page.

Working a job's checklist

A job's page shows its inline-editable name, a line reading Due: {date} - Status: {status}, and its checklist. Use Back to property to return to the property page.

Tick and untick steps

Click a step's checkbox to mark it done — it saves immediately and shows a strikethrough. Click again to undo. If saving fails, the checkbox reverts and shows an inline error.

Add, remove, and reorder steps

  • Add — use the Add a step field at the bottom of the checklist, then Add.
  • Remove — click a step's Remove button. This happens immediately, with no confirmation prompt.
  • Reorder — use the up/down buttons on each step; order is for display only and doesn't affect anything else.

If a job has no steps yet, the checklist reads: "No steps on this job."

Edit a step's text

Click the description to edit it inline, same as a property or job name. This is locked once that step is ticked, or once the whole job is marked complete.

Completing a job

The Complete job button stays disabled until every step is ticked and the job has at least one step. While disabled, you'll see: "All steps must be ticked before the job can be completed."

Once every step is checked, click Complete job (Completing… while it saves). The job then shows "Completed on {date and time}."

Completion is permanent There is no way to reopen or undo a completed job, and no confirmation dialog before you complete one — double-check the checklist first.

Step / step template descriptionUp to 500 characters
Schedule multiplier ("every N …")Whole number, 1 or greater
Schedule start dateRequired
Schedule end dateOptional; if set, must be after the start date
Completing a jobEvery existing step must be ticked, and there must be at least one step

Monthly and yearly schedules that land on a day that doesn't exist in a given month (e.g. the 31st) are clamped to that month's last valid day.

Good to know

  • Nothing big can be deleted. Properties, assets, recurring schedules, and jobs are all permanent once created — only individual checklist steps or step templates can be removed, and only while their parent is still active. Assets can be marked obsolete instead.
  • An asset link is set at creation time. There's no way to attach an asset to an existing job or schedule, or to move work from one asset to another — choose the asset when you create the work.
  • No sharing. Each Google account's properties and jobs are private to that account; there's no invite or collaborator feature.
  • No reminders or calendar view. The app doesn't send notifications — check back to see what's due.
  • Dates have no time component (except the timestamp recorded when you complete a job).
  • Everything saves as you go. There's no separate "save changes" step for the app as a whole, and — outside of what's listed above — no general undo.

Troubleshooting & FAQ

I can't find a delete button for a property or job — is that a bug?

No — it's by design. Properties, assets, recurring schedules, and jobs are permanent once created. Only individual steps can be removed.

I replaced my boiler — how do I get rid of the old asset?

Open the old asset's page and click Mark obsolete. It stops being offered for new work but keeps its badge, page, and full maintenance history. Create a new asset for the replacement. If you marked the wrong one, Reactivate undoes it.

The "Asset (optional)" dropdown is missing from the create form.

The dropdown only appears when the property has at least one asset that isn't obsolete. Add an asset in the property's Assets section first.

I edited a recurring schedule's steps, but a job I already generated didn't change.

Expected. Step template edits only apply to jobs generated after the edit; existing jobs keep their original steps.

The "Complete job" button is greyed out.

Tick every step first — the job also needs at least one step to ever be completable.

I got signed out or redirected to the sign-in page unexpectedly.

Your session expired. Sign in again with Google to pick up where you left off.

Can someone else help me manage the same property?

Not currently. Each property belongs to a single Google account, with no sharing or collaborator support.

Can I get a reminder before something is due?

Not yet — the app doesn't send notifications. Check the property page for what's due.
